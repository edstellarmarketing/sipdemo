The BIM & Digital Delivery department represents Arcova's highest-priority skills gap cluster. Across 49 assessed employees, the weighted gap score averages 1.4 — the widest in the organisation. Four skills have been flagged as critical: BIM (Advanced), AI & ML Literacy, IoT & Smart Infrastructure, and Digital Project Delivery. These gaps directly compromise Arcova's strategic goal of reducing third-party BIM dependency by 40% by end of FY2026.

The root cause is not talent deficit — it is skill vintage. 68% of department employees joined Arcova with BIM skills calibrated to 2018–2021 industry standards. The platform's tools and client expectations have advanced significantly. The gap is a velocity problem, not a capability problem.

The Dubai cluster (17 employees) carries the steepest gap score of 2.1 versus the department average of 1.8 — driven by three BIM Manager roles that are operating below the L3 proficiency required for the Dubai Roads Authority contract deliverables due Q3 2026. This is a delivery risk, not merely a development gap.

Recommended intervention sequence: Accelerate the AI & Data Literacy programme currently in progress — current completion at 57% is below the 75% cycle target. Commission an advanced BIM programme for the Dubai cluster. Fast-track IoT & Smart Infrastructure as a Q3 2026 intervention — no programme is currently scheduled and this skill is linked to 3 of Arcova's top 5 client deliverables.
